History

The Hazara people are an ethnic group native to the mountainous region of Hazarajat in central Afghanistan. They have a distinct culture and language, Hazaragi, which is a dialect of Persian.

The Hazara have a rich history, marked by periods of persecution and migration. More about their history can be found on the Wikipedia page.

Source: Wikimedia Commons

Culture

Hazara culture is a unique blend of Persian, Mongol, and Central Asian influences. They are known for their music, dance, and poetry.

The Nawruz festival, celebrated on the Persian New Year, is one of their most important cultural events. You can read more about Hazara culture on this cultural website.

Current Situation

Today, the Hazara people face significant challenges, including discrimination and violence. Despite these difficulties, they continue to strive for recognition and equality.

Various organizations are working to support the Hazara, and more information can be found on the Hazara support site.
